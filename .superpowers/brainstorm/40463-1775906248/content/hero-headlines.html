Hero — Headline options

Six directions. All riff on the same truth: you're wasting mornings, Morning fixes that. Cycling card stays the same throughout.

Option 1 — The direct trade

Morning

Less scrolling.
More knowing.

A handful of ideas from science, economics, psychology and history — delivered every morning, finished in minutes.

Health

Sleep is not rest. It's when your brain does its most important work.

MORNING

Feel: Punchy, symmetrical. "Less / More" is a clean trade everyone understands. No doomscrolling word — doesn't need it. The contrast is implied.

Option 2 — The call-out

Morning

You scroll for an hour.
You learn nothing.

Morning is five ideas worth knowing — from Health, Money, Tech, Psychology, and the World. Every day. Done in minutes.

Money

The market pays for what's hard to replace, not what's hard to do.

MORNING

Feel: Confrontational in a good way — names the problem the user already knows they have. "Nothing" lands hard. Most direct of the bunch.

Option 3 — The reframe

Morning

Your morning routine
was supposed to
include this.

One insight from across five domains of human knowledge. Delivered every morning. Finished before your coffee goes cold.

Tech

AI doesn't think. It predicts — and that distinction changes everything.

MORNING

Feel: Nostalgic and aspirational. Speaks to the person who knows they should be learning but doesn't. "Was supposed to" carries mild guilt without being preachy.

Option 4 — The stop/start

Morning

Doomscrolling.
Done.

Replace your morning scroll with five ideas worth keeping. Health, Money, Tech, Psychology, and the World — every day.

Psychology

You don't rise to the level of your goals. You fall to the level of your systems.

MORNING

Feel: Playful and punchy. Strikethrough on "Doomscrolling" is visually distinctive — makes the point without a sentence. "Done." is satisfying.

Option 5 — The habit framing

Morning

The five-minute habit
that actually makes
you smarter.

One insight from Health, Money, Tech, Psychology and the World — delivered every morning. No feed. No algorithm. Just things worth knowing.

World

Every border you see on a map was drawn by someone with an army.

MORNING

Feel: Bold claim, "actually" earns it some humility. "Five-minute" sets an expectation. Most benefit-driven — closest to a traditional product pitch. "Smarter" might feel oversold to some.

Option 6 — The minimalist

Morning

Know things.
On purpose.

Five curated ideas from Health, Money, Tech, Psychology and the World. Every morning. Finished before the rest of the internet gets to you.

Health

Sleep is not rest. It's when your brain does its most important work.

MORNING

Feel: Wry, a little dry. "On purpose" is the anti-doomscrolling message without using the word. Most brand-aligned with Morning's editorial, unhurried tone. Polarising — some will love it, some won't get it.
