Hero — Three directions

Headline, sub-line, and visual all redesigned. Each direction has a different personality.

Direction A — "Something worth knowing"

Morning

Something worth knowing.
Every morning.

One insight, drawn from science, economics, psychology and history. Finished before your coffee goes cold.

Headline feel

Quiet confidence. Doesn't explain what Morning is — promises what it delivers. The "Every morning" grounds the habit.

Visual

Single card cycling through all 5 categories — category strip changes colour, hook text fades in/out. Shows the breadth without the clutter of a stack.

Best for

Users who respond to "what's in it for me" immediately. The hook lines visible on the card do the selling.

Direction B — "A few minutes. A sharper mind."

Morning

A few minutes.
A sharper mind.

Five categories. One idea each. Knowledge that compounds — quietly, reliably, over years.

Money

The market pays for what's hard to replace, not what's hard to do.

Psychology

You don't rise to the level of your goals. You fall to the level of your systems.

Health

Sleep is not rest. It's when your brain does its most important work.

MORNING

Headline feel

Direct value proposition. "A few minutes" is honest (not "10 minutes"), "sharper mind" is the outcome. Clean two-part rhythm.

Visual

Three-card fanned stack. Back cards slide in on load with staggered delay. Front card gently floats. Feels alive without being busy.

Best for

The depth of the stack implies "there's more." The floating front card draws the eye and keeps the visual interesting over time.

Direction C — "Five worlds. One morning."

Morning

Five worlds.
One morning.

Health, Money, Tech, Psychology, World — one insight from each, every day. No feed. No algorithm. Just things worth knowing.

Health

Money

Tech

World

Psychology

Morning

Headline feel

"Five worlds. One morning." is the most structurally memorable. Short, symmetrical, explains the concept in four words.

Visual

Category constellation: 5 coloured dots connected to a central "Morning" node, lines drawing in on load. No card — more abstract, more minimal. Completely unique.

Best for

If the brand wants to feel more like a publication than a product. The visual is the most "editorial." But it doesn't show what a card looks like.
